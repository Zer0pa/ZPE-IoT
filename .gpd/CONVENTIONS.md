# Conventions

## Scope Conventions

- The sovereign truth surface is the latest reproducible authority artifact set, currently the March 20, 2026 managed preflight, strict DT, and real public E1 benchmark summary.
- Relative dates such as "today" or "latest" are not authoritative in artifacts; use explicit calendar dates.
- Stale project docs may be cited as evidence of drift, but never as live authority.

## Benchmark Conventions

- The governing benchmark surface is the unchanged real public E1 suite.
- Compression performance is reported as `CR` in `x` units.
- PT-6 is reported as `wins/total`, not as percentage-only prose.
- Dataset labels such as `DS-01` and `DS-05` must be preserved exactly.

## Evidence Conventions

- Auxiliary datasets such as Chemosense may inform diagnosis but cannot substitute for E1 authority results.
- Clean-environment claims require explicit evidence from a blind clone or equivalently clean workspace, plus wheel verification where relevant.
- Document polish is never treated as evidence of objective completion.

## Operational Conventions

- Keep artifacts compact because disk headroom is a real constraint in the current environment.
- Preserve unrelated dirty-worktree changes.
- Record unsupported claims explicitly instead of softening them into marketing language.

## Physics-Specific Note

This project is an engineering evidence and benchmarking workflow, so physics sign conventions and unit locks are not applicable here.
